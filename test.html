What does love feel like?

Love to me feels most like the feeling where you can be your true authentic self. Love is about being able to heal through traumas. Whether it’s love through someone else or self love. Love is about being able to still stay true to yourself and be able to give yourself love or someone else love without being codependent on another person to satisfy their needs for you to receive their love back.

Jordan 23 Female Bisexual

What does unconditional love mean to you, and have you experienced it?

Unconditional love to me is, quite literally, loving without conditions. That means knowing and accepting the entire person, even with all of their faults or mistakes. It means you won't give up on them or stop loving them no matter what, because we are all humans, and we all have our flaws and make mistakes. I have experienced it and I experience it now, with my partner. Even though he has seen me at my absolute lowest, he accepts it, seeks to understand it, empathizes with me, and loves me anyway.

Courtney 33 Cis Female Tulsa, OK

How do you nurture love in long-term friendships?

I nurture love in long-term friendships by staying connected, even if it's just through a quick text or phone call. I make an effort to remember important dates and milestones in their lives. I also believe in being there during both good times and bad, offering support and celebrating their achievements. Consistent communication and genuine care are key to maintaining strong, loving friendships.

Casey He/Him 37

How do you recover and heal from a broken heart?

Healing from a broken heart is a gradual process for me. I allow myself to feel and process the emotions rather than suppressing them. I lean on my support system, my friends and family, who remind me of my worth and keep me company. Engaging in activities I love, like painting and hiking, also helps me find joy and distraction. I practice self-compassion, reminding myself that it's okay to take time to heal and that it's a part of life.

Laura She/They 30s Poly Lesbian

What lessons have you learned from experiencing heartbreak or loss in love?

I've learned that love, even when it ends in heartbreak, is worth experiencing. It teaches us resilience and the capacity to deeply connect with others. Heartbreak has shown me the importance of cherishing moments and expressing love openly. It's also taught me the value of self-love and finding strength within myself. Healing comes with time, and with it, a renewed appreciation for the beauty of love and life.
