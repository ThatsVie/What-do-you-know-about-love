What do you know about love?

I’m learning that love isn’t about sacrificing yourself for someone else. It’s about being there, truly being present and supportive. I don’t want my child to grow up with the same patterns I did. I want her to know unconditional love, the kind that doesn’t come with strings attached or a fear of being left behind. I'm still figuring it out, but I know that real love should make you feel safe, valued, and understood. That's the kind of love I want to give my child, and maybe one day, I’ll fully understand and feel it for myself too.

How have your experiences of love evolved over time?

Love has become much simpler for me as I've aged. I used to think love was all about the grand gestures, the excitement, the passion. I made a lot of mistakes chasing those fleeting moments, and in the process, I caused harm to my children and was harmed by my husbands. Love seemed so complicated back then, and I often lost myself trying to keep up with what I thought love should be.

Now I realize that love is much simpler than I ever understood. It’s about being present, truly being there for the people you care about. Be here now. Always. Over and over. Don’t bend over backwards too much. Don’t lie. Don’t cheat. Be honest. Keep it simple. Life is already complicated enough.

I’ve learned to value honesty and consistency over anything else. I've made amends for the harm I've caused and I strive to do better every day. I’m grateful for my age because it has given me the perspective to not care as much about what other people think. I focus on being myself and being true to those I love. When you make mistakes, you make amends and you don't repeat them. That’s the essence of love for me now. It’s about being genuine, being accountable, and being present. Love is simple if you let it be.

What advice would you give to someone struggling to find or feel love?

Finding love can sometimes feel elusive, especially when you're struggling to feel it within yourself. Start by focusing on self-love, embracing your unique qualities, and practicing self-compassion. Understand your worth and radiate that confidence outward. Be patient, as love often arrives when you least expect it, and trust that it will find you when the time is right. Surround yourself with supportive friends and family who uplift you, and engage in activities that bring you joy. Doing things you love will make you happier and more fulfilled. Open up and be vulnerable, allowing others to see and love the real you. If you're struggling with feelings of unworthiness or past traumas, consider seeking help from a therapist, as professional guidance can help you work through issues that may be blocking you from experiencing love.
